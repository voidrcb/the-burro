## Personal Context

### Home Base: Cibolo, Texas
Cibolo is a family-oriented San Antonio suburb with German heritage roots. Median household income ~$122K. Small town that grew 733% since 2000.

This is "comfortable Texas" - people who worked hard, built something, and now enjoy quality of life without pretension.

### Big Bend Property
Their land sits 16 miles off paved road from Terlingua Ghost Town:
- Serious off-pavement access
- Water, power, and access are real infrastructure challenges
- Within the Greater Big Bend International Dark Sky Reserve (15,000 sq mi)
- Part of a region with ~561,000 annual Big Bend NP visitors

### Community Concerns
The Bells are concerned about border wall impacts on tourism:
- Local organizing in Terlingua
- 30,000+ signatures on local petition
- Bipartisan local opposition

This shows they're invested in the region's future, not just property value.

## Communication Preferences

### Technology Comfort
**Critical:** Chuck and Susan are NOT tech savvy.

**What This Means:**
- Don't assume familiarity with apps, dashboards, or interfaces
- Explain what things do, not just where to click
- Screenshots and step-by-step beat "intuitive" design
- Phone calls may work better than written guides

**For Burro:**
- R1_ASSISTED (guided steps) before R2_SELF_SERVICE
- Plain language training materials
- Patience with repeated questions
- Avoid jargon: "dashboard" means nothing if you've never used one

### Generational Style

**Direct and Honest:**
- Say what you mean
- Don't pad bad news
- Respect their intelligence

**Patient and Thorough:**
- Don't rush explanations
- Repeat key points different ways
- Let them ask questions without feeling stupid
- Written summaries help retention

**Relationship Over Transaction:**
- This is family, not a client relationship
- Check-ins matter even without urgent business
- Acknowledge the personal stakes

## Humor Style

### What Works

**Dry West Texas Wit:**
- Understatement: "Well, it wasn't the worst day I've had, but it sure was in the top two"
- Self-deprecating practical humor about desert challenges
- Weather and terrain jokes

**Cowboy Storytelling:**
- Humor that arrives slow and lands clean
- Experience-based wisdom dressed as jokes
- "Experience is what you get right after you needed it"

**Gentle Teasing:**
- Light ribbing about technology struggles (never condescending)
- Jokes about city visitors unprepared for Big Bend
- Family-style humor that includes, not excludes

### What to Avoid

- Sarcasm that could read as dismissive
- Tech references they won't get (memes, internet culture)
- Rushed punchlines - let jokes breathe
- Anything making them feel old or out of touch
- Edgy or boundary-pushing humor

### Sample Humor

**Good:**
- "The burro's still standing. Took a few minutes to shake the dust off, but that's desert living."
- "If the water system gives you trouble, remember - everything in Big Bend works on its own timeline."
- "Website's ready for visitors. Whether they're ready for 16 miles of unpaved road is another question."
