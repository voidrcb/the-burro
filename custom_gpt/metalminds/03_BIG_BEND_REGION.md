# Big Bend Region - Local Knowledge

## Geography

### Location
- Far West Texas, along the Rio Grande
- Brewster County - largest county in Texas (6,193 sq mi)
- 16 miles off paved road from Terlingua Ghost Town
- Remote: Nearest major city (El Paso or San Antonio) is 4+ hours

### Key Places
| Place | Distance | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|

### The Rio Grande
- Forms the US-Mexico border through the region
- Santa Elena Canyon - dramatic 1,500-foot walls
- Float trips possible year-round (water level dependent)
- Kayaking, canoeing, rafting available

## Dark Sky Reserve

### Greater Big Bend International Dark Sky Reserve
- **World's largest** International Dark Sky Reserve
- 15,000 square miles
- IDA (International Dark-Sky Association) certified
- Bortle Scale 1-2 (darkest possible)

### What This Means
- Milky Way visible to naked eye year-round
- Professional-quality astrophotography without travel
- Dark sky photography workshops command $700-8000/person
- True darkness is increasingly rare - this is the real thing

### Dark Sky Compliance
- All outdoor lighting must be:
  - Fully shielded (no light above horizontal)
  - 2700K or warmer color temperature
  - Only as bright as necessary
  - Off when not needed

## Weather and Climate

### Temperature Patterns
| Season | Day High | Night Low | Notes |
|--------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Winter (Dec-Feb) | 60-70°F | 30-40°F | Occasional freeze |
| Spring (Mar-May) | 75-90°F | 45-60°F | Best weather |
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | 95-105°F | 70-80°F | Very hot days |
| Fall (Sep-Nov) | 75-90°F | 50-60°F | Excellent |

### Key Weather Facts
- Summer heat exceeds 100°F by late morning (May-Sep)
- Monsoon season July-September brings afternoon storms
- Flash flood risk in canyons during storms
- Winter nights can freeze
- Low humidity year-round

### Seasonality Impact
- **Peak season:** March-April, November-December
- **Shoulder:** January-February, May, September-October
- **Low season:** June-August (too hot for daytime activities)

## Practical Considerations

### Access Challenges
- 16 miles unpaved road to property
- 4WD recommended, especially after rain
- Washboard roads common
- No cell service in many areas
- GPS may be unreliable

### Utilities
- **Water:** Hauled or private well
- **Power:** Grid extension ($5-25/foot) or off-grid solar
- **Septic:** OSSF systems required
- **Internet:** Satellite (Starlink) or unreliable cellular

### Emergency Services
- Fire/EMS response time: 30+ minutes
- Nearest hospital: Alpine (100 mi)
- Self-reliance is essential
- Know snake/scorpion first aid
