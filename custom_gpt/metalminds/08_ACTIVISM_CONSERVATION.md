# Activism & Conservation

## Overview

Big Bend Burro is committed to preserving the unique character of the Big Bend region. Conservation is not a marketing angle - it's central to why this place matters and why guests come here.

---

## Dark Sky Preservation

### Greater Big Bend International Dark Sky Reserve
- World's largest Dark Sky Reserve (15,000 sq mi)
- IDA (International Dark-Sky Association) certified
- Requires ongoing compliance and advocacy

### Compliance Requirements
All outdoor lighting must be:
- **Fully shielded:** No light above horizontal
- **Warm color:** 2700K or warmer
- **Minimal:** Only as bright as necessary
- **Controlled:** Off when not needed

### Why It Matters
- Darkest skies in continental US
- Milky Way visible to naked eye
- Supports astrotourism economy
- Wildlife depends on natural darkness
- Once lost, extremely difficult to restore

### Our Role
- All property lighting is dark-sky compliant
- Educate guests on light pollution
- Support local dark sky initiatives
- Document any non-compliant development nearby

## Border Wall Concerns

### Local Impact
The Big Bend region faces potential border wall construction that would:
- Obstruct open landscape views
- Damage tourism economy
- Disrupt wildlife corridors
- Change the character of the region

### Community Response
- Standing-room-only meetings at Terlingua's Cinnabar Theatre
- 30,000+ signatures on local petition
- Bipartisan local opposition
- Tourism businesses united in concern

### Our Position
- We support local preservation efforts
- We provide accurate information to guests who ask
- We don't force politics but don't hide our concerns
- This is about economics and environment, not partisanship

### How to Discuss with Guests
If asked:
> "The border wall debate is real here. Local businesses and residents - across party lines - are concerned about impacts on tourism and wildlife. If you want to learn more, the local preservation groups have good information."

Don't:
- Force the topic on guests
- Make it partisan
- Assume guest views
- Get into arguments

## Water Conservation

### Desert Reality
- Water is the limiting factor for all development
- Aquifer sustainability is a regional concern
- Hauled water costs $0.02-0.05/gallon
- Well drilling is $30-80+/foot with no guarantee

### Our Practices
- Low-flow fixtures throughout
- Greywater recycling where permitted
- Native, drought-tolerant landscaping
- Guest education on water use
- Rainwater capture systems

### Guest Communication
> "Water in Big Bend is precious. We ask guests to be mindful - short showers, report leaks, and appreciate that every drop matters out here."

## Wildlife Stewardship

### Local Wildlife
- Javelinas
- Mountain lions (rare but present)
- Black bears (occasional)
- Snakes (rattlesnakes, non-venomous species)
- Scorpions
- Roadrunners, quail, raptors
- 400+ bird species in region

### Guest Guidelines
- Don't feed wildlife
- Watch where you step (snakes, scorpions)
- Secure food and trash
- Respect animals' space
- Report injured wildlife

### Safety Basics
- **Snakes:** Watch where you put hands and feet. If bitten, seek medical care.
- **Scorpions:** Shake out shoes and clothes. Most stings are painful but not dangerous.
- **Javelinas:** Don't approach. They're not aggressive but will defend themselves.
- **Mountain lions:** Extremely rare encounters. Make noise, don't run, appear large.

## How to Talk About It

### Good Approach
- Lead with love of place, not criticism of others
- Share facts, not judgments
- Connect conservation to guest experience
- Make it practical, not preachy

### Phrases That Work
- "We're lucky to have the darkest skies in the country."
- "Water out here is life - we treat it that way."
- "The wildlife was here first. We're guests in their home."
- "This place is special. We want to keep it that way."

### Phrases to Avoid
- "You should..."
- "People don't understand..."
- "The problem is..."
- Anything preachy or condescending
